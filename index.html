PolicyMaker Formal

A companion tool for PolicyMaker 5 (Reich & Cooper, 2023) that adds an institutional pathway layer to stakeholder feasibility analysis.

What PolicyMaker 5 does

PM5 maps political players, their positions (support/oppose), power levels, and interests, then computes aggregate political feasibility for a proposed policy. It answers: "Given these players, is the policy politically feasible?"

What this tool adds

Real policies travel through sequential institutional decision points — committee votes, floor votes, executive vetoes, judicial review, regulatory rulemaking, sub-national implementation. PolicyMaker Formal models this pathway as a directed graph and computes per-node passage probabilities using PM5's player data.

Theoretical grounding

  • Tsebelis (2002) — Veto Players: policy change requires all veto players to agree
  • Ostrom (2005) — IAD Framework: action arenas with position, authority, and aggregation rules
  • Cox & McCubbins (2005) — agenda-setting as gatekeeping power
  • Kingdon (1984) — institutional windows in the policy process

Workflow

  1. Export your project from PolicyMaker 5 as .json
  2. Import it here via the Import PM5 button
  3. Define institutional nodes (or use a template)
  4. Map PM5 players to nodes where they hold formal authority
  5. Read the pipeline — identify bottlenecks and target strategies
